# 21.Roadmaps — Introductory Call: Presenter Notes

**Format:** 30-minute Google Meet video call with Gemini note-taker  
**Objective (visible):** Understand the prospect's world, co-envision what's next  
**Objective (hidden):** Probe and size willingness to pay; qualify the opportunity  
**Tone:** Consultative, advisory, educational — never sales-y

---

## Pre-Call Checklist

- [ ] Gemini note-taker enabled in Google Meet settings
- [ ] Slide deck open in a browser tab (not shared yet — share only when transitioning to Co-Envision)
- [ ] Replace `[Prospect Name]` and `[Date]` on slide 1
- [ ] Have the prospect's company website open for reference
- [ ] Pen and paper ready for your own notes (separate from Gemini)

---

## Slide 1 — Title (0:00 – 0:02) | 2 min

### What to say

> "Thank you for taking the time. I want to be upfront: this is your time, not mine. I'm here to understand your business, your challenges, and to think together about what's possible. No pitch, no pressure — just a conversation between two people who care about getting things right."

### Presenter guidance

- **Do NOT share the deck on screen yet.** Start camera-to-camera.
- Set the frame: you are the advisor, they are the expert on their business.
- If they brought their CTO, acknowledge them warmly but keep addressing the CEO/business leader as the primary.
- Establish psychological safety: "There are no wrong answers today."

### Hidden objective signals to watch for

- How they react to "no pitch, no pressure" — do they relax or stay guarded?
- Early body language: leaning in = engaged, arms crossed = skeptical.

---

## Slide 2 — Agenda (0:02 – 0:03) | 1 min

### What to say

> "Here's how I'd like to spend our time: about half listening to you — your business, your context — and the other half exploring together where you want to go. I'll share my screen briefly for a couple of visual anchors, but this is a conversation, not a presentation. Sound good?"

### Presenter guidance

- **Share screen briefly** to show this slide, then stop sharing.
- Get explicit verbal agreement on the format — this creates a micro-commitment.
- If they say "Actually, I'd like to know more about what you do first," redirect gently: "Absolutely — and the best way I can show you what we do is by doing it. Let me start by understanding your world."

---

## Slide 3 — Your World (0:03 – 0:08) | ~5 min

### What to say

> "So, tell me about your business. What do you do, who are your customers, and what does your competitive landscape look like right now?"

### Key follow-up questions

- "Where are you winning today?"
- "What's changed in the last 12 months that's making things harder?"
- "If I talked to your best customer, what would they say you do better than anyone?"
- "And what's the one thing they'd say you need to fix?"

### Presenter guidance

- **Do NOT share this slide.** This is your conversation guide — keep it off-screen.
- Let them talk. Resist the urge to fill silence — count to 5 before jumping in.
- Listen for: margin compression signals, customer churn hints, growth ambitions, technology frustrations.
- Take mental notes on their vocabulary — mirror it back later.

### Hidden objective signals

- **Willingness to invest:** Listen for language like "we've been looking at...", "we tried...", "we budgeted for..." — these signal prior spend appetite.
- **Urgency level:** "We need to move fast" vs. "We're exploring" — this sizes the deal timeline.
- **Decision authority:** "I decide" vs. "I need to talk to my board" — this tells you if you're talking to the buyer.

---

## Slide 4 — AI & Cyber in Your Reality (0:08 – 0:15) | ~7 min

### What to say (transition)

> "That's really helpful context. Let me zoom in on two forces that are reshaping every business we talk to — and I'd love to understand how they show up in yours."

### AI-side questions (pick 2-3, don't interrogate)

- "When you think about AI, what comes to mind for your business? Opportunity? Threat? Both?"
- "Are your competitors doing things with AI that worry you?"
- "Have you tried anything — even experiments? What happened?"
- "What's the biggest blocker to doing more with AI?"

### Cyber-side questions (pick 2-3)

- "How connected is your business today? Cloud, SaaS, remote workforce?"
- "Have you experienced any security incidents — even small ones?"
- "Where does cybersecurity sit in your organization? Who owns it?"
- "If something happened tomorrow — a breach, a ransomware attack — how confident are you in your response?"

### Presenter guidance

- **Share screen briefly** to show this slide as a visual anchor, then stop sharing.
- This is where the call gets educational. After they answer, reflect back with a pattern:
  - "What you're describing is very common in [their industry]. The challenge is that..."
  - "That's actually a signal of [X]. Here's why that matters..."
- **This is your advisory moment.** Give them one genuine insight they didn't have before. Examples:
  - "The fact that your competitors are using AI to [X] means your margin gap will accelerate, not stabilize."
  - "Having no dedicated cyber owner is itself the biggest risk — because it means no one is watching the attack surface grow."

### Hidden objective signals

- **Budget signals:** "We hired a consultant for..." / "We invested in [tool]..." / "Our IT budget is..."
- **Pain intensity:** The more specific their examples, the more urgent the need.
- **Sophistication level:** How they talk about AI/Cyber tells you if they need foundational or advanced guidance — this sizes which tier they'd enter at.

---

## Slide 5 — Quick Readiness Pulse (0:15 – 0:20) | ~5 min

### What to say (transition)

> "I'd love to try something quick with you. I have a simple self-assessment — 7 dimensions, takes about 2 minutes. There are no grades, no scores. It's just a way to make our conversation more concrete. Can I share my screen?"

### Presenter guidance

- **Share screen** and keep it shared through slides 5, 6, and 7.
- Walk through each row. Read the dimension name, then ask: "Where would you place yourself?"
- **Do NOT fill in the circles yourself** during the live call — just note their answers verbally. (You'll fill them in for the summary report.)
- If they hesitate on a row, that hesitation IS the insight. Say: "The fact that it's hard to place is actually telling — it usually means there's no formalized approach yet, which puts you somewhere between Not Started and Ad Hoc."
- After completing the table, pause and reflect: "So here's what I'm seeing..."

### The educational moment

This is where you deliver free value. Patterns to call out:

- **AI high, Cyber low:** "This is a common and dangerous pattern. You're building on AI without the security foundation to protect it. Every AI capability you add also adds attack surface."
- **Everything Ad Hoc:** "There's nothing wrong with Ad Hoc — it means you've started. But Ad Hoc doesn't scale. The jump from Ad Hoc to Emerging is where most organizations get stuck, because it requires capabilities, not just tools."
- **Cyber high, AI low:** "You've invested in protecting the house, but you're not yet building the rooms that will generate new revenue. The good news: your security foundation accelerates AI adoption."

### Hidden objective signals

- **Self-awareness level:** Leaders who are honest about "Not Started" are more likely to buy. Overconfident self-assessments signal a harder sell.
- **Where they cluster:** If most answers land on "Not Started / Ad Hoc," they need foundational work (longer engagement, higher value). If "Emerging / Structured," they need operational work (targeted, faster).

---

## Slide 6 — As-Is / To-Be Canvas (0:20 – 0:25) | ~5 min

### What to say (transition)

> "Now let's put this together. Based on everything you've shared, I'd like to map the gap between where you are today and where you'd like to be in 12 to 18 months. I'll guide you through it."

### How to facilitate each row

Walk through each dimension. For each:
1. **Summarize the As-Is** in their own words: "So today, your AI capabilities are essentially [X] — is that fair?"
2. **Ask for the To-Be:** "And where would you like to be in 12-18 months on this?"
3. **Note the gap:** "That's a meaningful gap. The question is: what needs to happen to close it?"

### Presenter guidance

- Type live into the canvas as they speak (or annotate with a drawing tool).
- This is the most powerful slide in the deck — it makes the need tangible and visual.
- **Key advisory moment:** After filling 3-4 rows, pause and say:
  - "Look at the pattern here. The gaps aren't independent — your AI ambition depends on your Cloud foundation, which depends on your Cyber posture. They're interconnected."
  - This is the educational setup for the 21 Roadmaps framework — but you do NOT name it or pitch it. Let the insight land on its own.

### Hidden objective signals

- **Ambition level of the To-Be:** Ambitious targets = larger engagement potential.
- **Specificity:** Vague To-Be answers = early-stage thinking (Tier 1 candidate). Specific targets = ready to commit (Tier 2 candidate).
- **Emotional investment:** If they lean in and start completing rows themselves, they're already co-creating — this is a strong buying signal.

---

## Slide 7 — Emerging Picture (0:25 – 0:28) | ~3 min

### What to say

> "Let me reflect back what I'm hearing — and share a couple of thoughts."

### Presenter guidance

- Summarize the 3 strongest insights from the conversation. Use THEIR words, not yours.
- For each insight, pair it with a direction — this is your advisory value:
  - "You said [X]. What that tells me is [Y]. One direction worth exploring is [Z]."
- **This is where the prospect should think: "This person understands my business."**
- The quote at the bottom — "The gap between where you are and where you need to be is your roadmap" — read it aloud if the moment feels right. It plants the seed without pitching.

### Do NOT

- Mention the 21 Roadmaps framework by name
- Mention pricing, tiers, or engagement structure
- Say "we can help you with that" — instead say "this is solvable" or "organizations that address this typically..."

---

## Slide 8 — Close (0:28 – 0:30) | ~2 min

### What to say

> "Thank you — genuinely. You've been very open today, and that takes trust.
>
> Here's what happens next: within 48 hours, I'll send you a written summary of what we discussed today — the diagnostic, the As-Is / To-Be canvas, and a few tailored recommendations. It's yours to keep, no strings attached.
>
> After that, the ball is entirely in your court. If you'd like to continue the conversation, I'm here. If not, no hard feelings — you'll still walk away with something useful."

### Presenter guidance

- **Stop sharing your screen** before this slide. Go back to camera-to-camera.
- The 48h summary is your re-engagement tool — it gives you a reason to follow up.
- "No strings attached" reinforces the no-pressure frame and builds trust.
- If they ask "What would the next step look like?" — this is the golden question. Answer with:
  - "Typically, the next step would be a focused workshop session where we take one of these dimensions — whichever is most urgent for you — and build out a concrete roadmap. It's about 2 hours, and you'd walk away with something actionable. Want me to include that as an option in the summary?"

### Hidden objective: final qualification

- **Strong buy signal:** They ask about next steps, timelines, or cost unprompted.
- **Medium signal:** They say "This was really helpful, let me think about it."
- **Weak signal:** They say "Thanks, we'll be in touch" with no specifics.
- If strong signal, you can mention Tier 1 lightly: "The way we typically start is with a single workshop session — low commitment, high value. I'll include the details."

---

## Post-Call Actions

1. **Within 1 hour:** Review Gemini notes, add your own observations and qualification signals
2. **Within 48 hours:** Send the summary report with:
   - Diagnostic results (fill in the circles on slide 5)
   - As-Is / To-Be canvas (cleaned up from slide 6)
   - 3 tailored recommendations
   - Optional: link to book a follow-up / first workshop session
3. **Day 5:** If no response, send a brief check-in referencing one specific insight from the call
4. **Day 10:** Final gentle follow-up, then move to nurture

---

## Gemini Note-Taker Instructions

Set the following prompt for Gemini before the call begins:

> "Please capture structured notes with the following sections:
> 1. **Prospect Context** — company description, industry, size, competitive landscape
> 2. **Business Pressures** — margin compression, customer challenges, growth constraints
> 3. **AI Reality** — current state, experiments, blockers, competitor activity
> 4. **Cyber Reality** — current posture, incidents, ownership, concerns
> 5. **Diagnostic Answers** — for each of the 7 dimensions, note the maturity level chosen
> 6. **As-Is / To-Be** — capture each dimension's current and target state
> 7. **Key Quotes** — verbatim quotes that reveal pain intensity or buying signals
> 8. **Action Items** — any commitments made by either party"
